踩踏事件具有可预见性 踩踏事件的应对措施 踩踏事件的危害

科普：当踩踏事件发生时，我们应该怎么做？

Yuqi Zhang BFSU SEIS


10月31日，韩国首尔，不少民众自发前往梨泰院踩踏事件现场附近的街道吊唁遇难者。两天前，这里曾有超过10万人参加聚会，庆祝即将到来的万圣节。当时，聚会地点附近某酒店旁狭窄下行的胡同内人山人海，由于前方跌倒而后方人士不知情地跟进，导致严重的踩踏事故，庆祝狂欢变成了呼喊求救。

On October 31, many people in Seoul, South Korea, went to the street near the site of the Itaewon stampede to pay their respects to the victims. Two days ago, more than 100,000 people gathered here to celebrate the upcoming Halloween. At that time, a crowd of people in a narrow alley near a hotel where the party was held fell down and people behind unknowingly followed, causing a serious stampede and turning the celebration into a cry for help.


据韩国警方统计，截至10月31日上午6时，梨泰院踩踏事故已造成154人死亡、33人重伤、116人轻伤。由于踩踏导致的伤亡事件不是个案。2014年12月31日，上海外滩迎接游客跨年活动中，黄浦江观景平台人行通道阶梯处底部突然有人失衡跌倒，继而引发多人摔倒、叠压，致使拥挤踩踏事件发生，造成36人死亡、49人受伤。

According to the police, 154 people were killed, 33 seriously injured and 116 lightly injured in the Itaewon stampede as of 6 a.m. on Oct. 31. Deaths and injuries caused by stampedes are not isolated incidents. On December 31, 2014, during the New Year's Eve activity on the Bund in Shanghai, someone suddenly fell unbalance at the bottom of the steps of the pedestrian walkway on the Huangpu River viewing platform, causing many people to fall and pile on top of each other, resulting in a stampede incident, resulting in 36 deaths and 49 injuries.


公共安全悲剧为何会发生？群体性活动安全措施如何保障？踩踏发生后如何自救？

Why do public safety tragedies happen? How to ensure the safety of group activities? How to save oneself after stampede?


有专业人士表示，与自然灾害不同，踩踏事件具有一定可预见性，主办方的事前预案、人员维护、救援装备准备得当，完全可以降低踩踏事件的发生。

Some professionals said that, unlike natural disasters, stampedes have certain predictability, and the organizers' pre-plan, personnel maintenance and rescue equipment are properly prepared, which can completely reduce the occurrence of stampedes.
